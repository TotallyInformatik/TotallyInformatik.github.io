  • 5th Apr 2020

    Hey everybody! I am back! I took a while off because I had lots of things to do in school. But from now on I will try to post as much as possible on this website! During corona time I have made a lot of progress in programming (in my opinion), so let me share with you one of the programes I have written.

    So now that the coronavirus exists, my school also had to shut down. We are currently receiving our education online via a platform called Moodle. You might know that website. Anyways, learning online has not been a good experience for me. We only receive tasks by our teachers and we have to do them and submit the tasks. Since we are doing our tasks from home, we can do them whenever we want to, so that means I have to plan my day. Even though I am not bad at organizing my day (maybe even one of the best in my grade), I just couldn't get myself to plan anything or do any of the tasks. I found that the main problem was that, when doing the excercises, I was constantly thinking about programming or doing something with the computer. Anything, except for doing the excercises I was suppossed to do. Normaly, I am very disciplined, I don't want to play games, I do the things that I have to do even if i don't want to do it, but now, it is as if I had lost all motivation. I just wanted to work on programming projects, problems etc.

    If that wasn't enough, the Moodle website or the way my teachers have been using this website to educate us has not helped me plan my day at all. The tasks are not organized, tasks you have completed still remain and often you forget what you've done and what you haven't, because teachers will sometimes add more excercises in the middle of the week! In order to help myself, I decided to program an executable which reads off of the Moodle website and only shows me the tasks I haven't done. This is done with a calendar system that displays every day that has a task due, red. That way, I can plan days more efficiently. When you click on those days, you see a list of excercises that are due and the exact times they are due! You can click a button that redirects you to a site that gives a more indepth description of that exercise and where you can submit your tasks!

    I have also implemented a feature where you can add your own tasks. Now I can plan my day on my computer and see all of the tasks that are actually relevant in one program!

    Now you might say: "but Rui! There is a calendar on Moodle already that displays all of the tasks! Your work is useless!" But listen. That calendar has it's own flaws too, at least in my own opinion! The calendar for example doesn't show what day it is! Since I am that kind of person who sometimes forgets what day it is and really doesn't care about it either. Knowing what day it is is essential for a calendar that should make organization easier, because if the current date were highlighted on that calendar, I would have a much better idea of what tasks are the most urgent!

    Another problem is that the tasks that Í have already submitted are still shown!!! That way I do not know whether or not a day is highlighted because there are tasks teachers have added that I actually have to do, or if that day is only highlighted because Moodle doesn't automatically delete tasks that you have done!

    Regardless of whether or not this application is useful for everybody (and if it is for many, I will probably upload it onto this website to allow you to download it), I had a lot of fun programming it and for me it is actually quite useful! I learned a lot of things (tkinter, beautifulsoup4, datetime, requests, etc.) and I found out a lot of stuff I can work on in my next project! (I have to focus on making a program that has an easily modifiable structure and that has readable code)

    Anyways, that is it for now! If you still don't understand the program you can head over to my Youtube channel TotallyInformatik where I demonstrate how it works. I have also programed an algorithm that finds the path through a maze and solved a few "hard" programming problems (according to a coding problem subscription). But since this blog post is already quite long and I should really go to bed, I will be talking about it in another blog post! There are also going to be videos on my Youtube channel concerning these programes!
